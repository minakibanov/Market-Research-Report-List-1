<p><p>The high temperature MLCC market is categorized into three main types based on dielectric properties. Class I ceramic dielectrics offer stable capacitance across temperature and voltage variations, making them ideal for precision applications. Class II ceramic dielectrics provide higher capacitance values but exhibit increased variance with temperature and voltage fluctuations, suitable for general-purpose applications. Class III ceramic dielectrics, designed for low-cost solutions, have significant capacitance changes and are typically used in less demanding environments.</p></p>
