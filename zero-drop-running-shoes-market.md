<p><p>Zero-drop running shoes are designed with a heel-to-toe drop of zero millimeters, meaning that the height of the heel is the same as that of the forefoot. This promotes a more natural foot position and encourages a forefoot or midfoot strike, which can help reduce the risk of injury and improve running efficiency. The rising awareness of health and wellness, combined with a growing preference for minimalist footwear, has led to increasing popularity in zero-drop running shoes.</p><p>The Zero-Drop Running Shoes Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.6% during the forecast period. Furthermore, social media and influencer partnerships are enhancing brand visibility and consumer engagement, further propelling market expansion.
