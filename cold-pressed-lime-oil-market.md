<p><p>The cold-pressed lime oil market features two primary types: Mexican lime (Citrus Aurantifolia) and Tahitian lime (Citrus Latifolia). Mexican lime, known for its strong aroma and high acidity, is favored in culinary applications and beverages, often used in cocktails and marinades. Tahitian lime, larger and less acidic, is popular for its milder flavor, commonly found in cooking and aromatherapy. Both types cater to diverse industries, including food and beverages, cosmetics, and essential oils, contributing to their market growth.</p></p>
