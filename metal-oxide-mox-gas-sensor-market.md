<p><p>Metal-Oxide (MOX) gas sensors are devices that detect and quantify gaseous substances by measuring changes in electrical resistance in a metal oxide layer, typically made of materials like tin oxide. These sensors are widely used for detecting various gases such as carbon monoxide, methane, and volatile organic compounds due to their high sensitivity, low cost, and compact size.
