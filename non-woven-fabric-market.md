<p><p>The non-woven fabric market is segmented into several types based on production methods. Meltblown fabrics are known for their fine fibers and filtration capabilities, commonly used in medical masks. Spunbonded fabrics are durable and versatile, widely utilized in hygiene products. Spunlace fabrics feature soft textures, ideal for wipes. Needle punch fabrics are created through mechanical entangling, suitable for carpet backing and geotextiles. Other types may include hydroentangled and flash spun fabrics, catering to specific industrial and consumer applications.</p></p>
